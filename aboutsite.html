Reflection
on My Site

When I first started designing my website, I didn't have a clear overall plan, which became evident as I progressed from Assignment 1 to Assignment 3. Hereâ€™s what I learned from iterating my design through the different versions:

Reusable Module: In the early stages, I didn't fully consider module reusability, especially the CSS. I focused only on the components I was developing without considering their impact on other pages. To address this, I redesigned the CSS structure, separating common styles from page-specific ones, making the site easier to maintain.

Friction Conversion: Calls-to-Action (CTA) initially lacked appeal and visibility. After feedback from Assignment 2 grading and peer reviews, I I changed the background of CTA buttons "Explore more" from transparent to white with opacity of 0.8 to make it more clearer and more attractive. I also optimized confusing labels. For instance, the previously non-clickable "What are you looking for" field was made clickable and editable, providing visitors with a more direct way to interact with the site.

Accessibility: Initially, I neglected the needs of people with disabilities, such as not including alternative text and having insufficient contrast. I added alternative text to images and ensured all href links have associated aria-labels. Additionally, abbreviations now include abbr tags, and I improved the visual contrast to ensure clear page structure. These changes have made the site more accessible to a broader audience.

Due to time constraints, there are still some tasks that remain unfinished. I plan to address these in future iterations:

Responsive Design: Initially, my website was designed only for my Chrome browser without considering other devices, screen widths, or resolutions. I redesigned the layout to ensure proper display across different devices, improving the website's responsiveness. However, I plan to continue refining this aspect.

Unfinished Content: Time constraints led me to comment out some features. The Beans module, for instance, was initially left incomplete. My plan is to divide this module into three sub-pages: Varieties, Processes, and Sensory, providing a comprehensive exploration of each aspect.
